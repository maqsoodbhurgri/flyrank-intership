# AI Workflow Audit

## Overview

This workflow audit analyzes recurring tasks and identifies where Artificial Intelligence can be used effectively, where human involvement is necessary, and where collaboration between humans and AI provides the best results.

The objective of this audit is to improve productivity, build repeatable workflows, and understand the strengths and limitations of AI tools.

---

# Weekly Recurring Tasks

| # | Task | Frequency | Classification | Rationale |
|---|------|------------|----------------|------------|
| 1 | Reading course materials and lecture notes | Daily | Just Me | Understanding and retaining knowledge requires personal effort and critical thinking. |
| 2 | Creating weekly study schedules | Weekly | Fully Automate | AI can generate structured schedules and reminders efficiently. |
| 3 | Summarizing articles and documentation | Weekly | Delegate to AI with Review | AI can summarize content quickly, but accuracy should be verified. |
| 4 | Researching technical concepts | Daily | Collaborate with AI | AI can explain concepts while the user validates and applies the information. |
| 5 | Preparing presentation outlines | Weekly | Delegate to AI with Review | AI can create an initial structure, while the final content requires human review. |
| 6 | Writing documentation and reports | Weekly | Delegate to AI with Review | AI can draft documents, but formatting and fact-checking are still necessary. |
| 7 | Debugging code and troubleshooting errors | Weekly | Collaborate with AI | AI can suggest possible solutions, but implementation and testing remain human responsibilities. |
| 8 | Organizing files and notes | Weekly | Fully Automate | Repetitive organization tasks can be automated effectively. |
| 9 | Creating practice quizzes and questions | Weekly | Fully Automate | AI can generate exercises quickly from study materials. |
|10| Reviewing assignment requirements | Weekly | Collaborate with AI | AI can help interpret instructions and identify missing requirements. |

|12| Setting long-term goals and priorities | Monthly | Just Me | Personal goals and decisions should remain human-driven. |

# Claude Project Details

### Purpose
This project is designed to:

- Improve productivity using AI tools.

- Identify tasks suitable for AI, humans, or collaborative approaches.

- Encourage verification and critical thinking while using AI.

---

# Three Target Tasks and Success Definitions

## Task 1: Research and Summarization

### Description
Use AI to summarize articles, study materials, and documentation.

### Done Well Means

- Main ideas are captured accurately.
- Important information is not omitted.
- Results are generated within 10 minutes.
- Less than 20% manual corrections are required.

---

## Task 2: Documentation and Report Drafting

### Description
Use AI to create first drafts for reports, notes, and structured documents.

### Done Well Means

- Content is logically organized.
- Grammar and formatting are professional.
- Less than 25% manual editing is required.
- Final draft is completed within 30 minutes.

---

## Task 3: Technical Problem Solving and Troubleshooting

### Description
Use AI to assist in identifying technical issues and suggesting solutions.

### Done Well Means

- Root cause is identified correctly.
- Suggested solutions are technically accurate.
- Solutions can be implemented successfully.
- Issues are resolved within 30 minutes.

---

# Key Learnings

- AI performs well in repetitive and structured tasks.
- Human judgment remains essential for decision-making and verification.
- Collaboration between humans and AI provides the best results for research, writing, and problem-solving tasks.
- Effective AI usage requires critical thinking, validation, and clearly defined workflows.
